Samosa recipe

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1/4 cup ghee (clarified butter) or vegetable oil
  • Cold water, as needed
  • 2 medium potatoes, boiled, peeled, and mashed
  • 1/2 cup green peas, boiled
  • 1 small onion, finely chopped
  • 2 green chilies, finely chopped
  • 1 teaspoon grated ginger
  • 1 teaspoon cumin seeds
  • 1 teaspoon coriander powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon garam masala
  • 1/2 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • Salt to taste
  • 2 tablespoons oil for frying

Instructions:

  1. Start by making the dough:
    1. In a mixing bowl, combine the all-purpose flour and salt.
    2. Add ghee or vegetable oil and mix it well with the flour until the mixture resembles breadcrumbs.
    3. Gradually add cold water while kneading the dough until it forms a smooth and firm consistency.
    4. Cover the dough with a damp cloth and let it rest for about 20-30 minutes.
  2. Prepare the filling:
    1. In a pan, heat 2 tablespoons of oil over medium heat.
    2. Add cumin seeds and let them splutter.
    3. Add chopped onions and sauté until they turn translucent.
    4. Add grated ginger and chopped green chilies, and sauté for a minute.
    5. Add boiled and mashed potatoes, boiled green peas, coriander powder, garam masala, turmeric powder, and salt to taste.
    6. Mix well and cook for a few minutes until the filling is well combined and heated through.
    7. Remove from heat and let the filling cool down.
  3. Shape the samosas:
    1. Take a small portion of the dough and roll it into a thin circle.
    2. Cut the circle in half.
    3. Take one half and fold it into a cone shape, overlapping the edges.
    4. Fill the cone with the prepared filling.
    5. Moisten the edges of the cone with water and press to seal the samosa.
    6. Repeat the process with the remaining dough and filling.
  4. Fry the samosas:
    1. In a deep pan or pot, heat oil for frying over medium heat.
    2. Add the shaped samosas to the hot oil and fry them until they turn golden brown and crispy.
    3. Remove the fried samosas from the oil and drain them on a paper towel to remove excess oil.
  5. Serve and enjoy:
    1. Serve the hot and crispy samosas with your favorite chutney or sauce.
    2. Enjoy them as a snack or as a delicious appetizer!
