Chips recipe

Ingredients:

  • 2 large potatoes
  • Vegetable oil, for frying
  • Salt, to taste
  • Optional: Seasonings of your choice (such as paprika, garlic powder, onion powder, or chili powder)

Instructions:

  1. Prepare the potatoes:
    1. Wash the potatoes thoroughly to remove any dirt.
    2. Peel the potatoes if desired, or leave the skins on.
    3. Slice the potatoes into thin, even slices using a knife or mandoline slicer.
  2. Rinse and dry the potato slices:
    1. Rinse the potato slices in cold water to remove excess starch.
    2. Pat them dry using a clean kitchen towel or paper towels. Ensure the slices are completely dry before frying.
  3. Fry the potato chips:
    1. Heat vegetable oil in a deep pan or pot over medium-high heat. The oil should be about 1-2 inches (2.5-5 cm) deep.
    2. Test the oil temperature by dropping a small potato slice into the hot oil. If it sizzles and starts frying immediately, the oil is ready.
    3. Carefully add a handful of potato slices to the hot oil, making sure not to overcrowd the pan.
    4. Fry the chips for about 3-5 minutes or until they turn golden brown and crispy. Stir occasionally for even cooking.
    5. Use a slotted spoon or tongs to remove the chips from the oil and transfer them to a paper towel-lined plate to drain excess oil.
  4. Season the chips:
    1. While the chips are still hot, sprinkle them with salt and any additional seasonings you prefer. Toss the chips gently to evenly distribute the seasonings.
  5. Repeat the frying process:
    1. Continue frying the remaining potato slices in batches until all the chips are cooked.
    2. Monitor the oil temperature and adjust the heat as needed to maintain a consistent frying temperature.
  6. Serve and enjoy:
    1. Once all the chips are cooked and seasoned, let them cool down for a few minutes.
    2. Transfer the homemade potato chips to a serving bowl or plate.
    3. Serve them immediately as a tasty snack or as a side dish with sandwiches or burgers.
