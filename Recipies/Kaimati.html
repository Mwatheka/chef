Kaimati Recipe

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1/4 cup sugar
  • 1 teaspoon instant yeast
  • 1/4 teaspoon ground cardamom
  • Pinch of salt
  • 1 cup warm water
  • Vegetable oil for deep frying
  • Honey or syrup for drizzling

Instructions:

  1. In a large mixing bowl, combine the all-purpose flour, sugar, instant yeast, ground cardamom, and salt.
  2. Add the warm water gradually while stirring the mixture with a spoon. Mix well until you have a smooth batter. The consistency should be thick but pourable.
  3. Cover the bowl with a clean kitchen towel and let the batter rest in a warm place for about 1-2 hours, or until it doubles in size.
  4. Heat vegetable oil in a deep pan or skillet over medium heat.
  5. Gently stir the batter to deflate it slightly. Using a tablespoon, drop small portions of the batter into the hot oil, creating round or irregular-shaped fritters. Fry them in batches, but do not overcrowd the pan.
  6. Fry the Kaimati until they turn golden brown, flipping them occasionally to ensure even cooking. This usually takes about 2-3 minutes per batch.
  7. Using a slotted spoon, transfer the fried Kaimati onto a paper towel-lined plate to drain excess oil.
  8. Drizzle the Kaimati with honey or syrup while they are still warm.
  9. Allow the Kaimati to cool slightly before serving. They are best enjoyed fresh and warm.

Enjoy your sweet and crispy Kaimati!
