Waru Bhajia

Ingredients:

  • 2 large potatoes
  • 1 cup chickpea flour (besan)
  • 1/4 cup rice flour
  • 1 teaspoon cumin seeds
  • 1/2 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon red chili powder (adjust to taste)
  • 1 teaspoon coriander powder
  • 1 teaspoon baking powder
  • Salt to taste
  • Water (as needed for the batter)
  • Oil for deep frying

Instructions

  1. Peel the potatoes and slice them into thin rounds or matchstick shapes. Place them in a bowl of cold water to prevent browning and set aside.
  2. In a mixing bowl, combine the chickpea flour, rice flour, cumin seeds, turmeric powder, red chili powder, coriander powder, baking powder, and salt. Mix well.
  3. Gradually add water to the dry ingredients, whisking continuously, until you achieve a smooth and thick batter consistency. The batter should be thick enough to coat the potato slices.
  4. Heat oil in a deep frying pan or pot over medium heat.
  5. Drain the potato slices and pat them dry with a kitchen towel to remove excess water.
  6. Dip each potato slice into the prepared batter, ensuring it is coated on all sides. Allow any excess batter to drip off.
  7. Carefully place the coated potato slices into the hot oil, a few at a time, without overcrowding the pan. Fry them until they turn golden brown and crispy, flipping them occasionally to cook evenly.
  8. Once fried, remove the bhajias using a slotted spoon and place them on a paper towel-lined plate to absorb any excess oil.
  9. Repeat the process with the remaining potato slices until all the bhajias are fried.
  10. Serve the Waru Bhajia hot with your favorite chutney, ketchup, or tamarind sauce for dipping.
